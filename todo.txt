Improve sentiment keyword scoring
----------------------------------
Current approach uses flat keyword sets (bullish/bearish) matched against
individual words in headlines. This misses context:
  - "falls short of record highs" scores neutral (bullish + bearish words cancel)
  - Negation is ignored ("not profitable" matches "profitable" as bullish)
  - No weighting (strong signals like "crash" equal to mild ones like "risk")

Options:
  1. VADER (nltk.sentiment) -- rule-based, no model download, handles negation
     and intensifiers out of the box. Lightweight, good fit.
  2. Bigram/trigram patterns -- e.g. "falls short" = bearish, "beats expectations"
     = bullish. Manual but precise for financial headlines.
  3. FinBERT or similar transformer -- highest accuracy but heavy dependency,
     slow inference, overkill for a side indicator.

Recommendation: start with VADER, fall back to current keywords if nltk
is not installed.
