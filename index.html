How the rating works

Each stock is scored on 12 financial KPIs (P/E, Forward P/E, P/B, EV/EBITDA, Debt/Equity, ROE, Profit Margin, Revenue Growth, Current Ratio, Dividend Yield, FCF Yield, PEG Ratio). Each KPI receives three sub-scores: an absolute score (how does the value compare to dynamic sector-relative thresholds based on the 20th/80th percentile of peers?), a relative score (how does it compare to the industry or sector median among peers?), and a trend score (is the metric improving or deteriorating based on annual financial statements?). The final rating is a weighted combination: 35% absolute + 55% relative + 10% trend. When trend data is unavailable for a KPI, its weight redistributes proportionally across absolute and relative. When industry peers are available (5+), the relative score uses industry comparison instead of the broader sector. Some KPIs with negative values are excluded (e.g. negative P/E) or penalized (e.g. negative book value), depending on whether the negative is meaningless or a red flag. KPIs are weighted by importance. The result is mapped to a 1-10 scale where 10 = best value and 1 = worst value.
